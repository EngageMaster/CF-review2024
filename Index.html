Cystic Fibrosis Year in Review 2024

Main Author: Amel Alameeri

Department of adult Pulmonary medicine, ST Shaikh Tahnoon medical City Hospital, Alain, AbuDhabi, UAE

Abstract

Keywords: Cystic fibrosis

The year 2024 marks a pivotal moment in the field of cystic fibrosis (CF) treatment. It's characterised by significant advancements in clinical care and an expanding body of literature on CF transmembrane conductance regulator (CFTR) modulators.

These CFTR therapies have transformed the landscape of CF management, offered systemic benefits, and established new guidelines for assessing clinical manifestations and therapies.

Additionally, progress has been made in newborn screening (NBS), diagnosis, and understanding outcomes for individuals with CF-related metabolic syndrome or inconclusive diagnostic results.

However, amidst these clinical milestones, disparities in global access to CFTR modulators (CFTRm) persist. This threatens to exacerbate existing inequities in CF care. This review provides a focused overview of the most impactful articles from 2024, highlighting both clinical advancements and pressing global accessibility challenges.

2024: A Pivotal Year for CF

The year 2024 marked a transformative era in cystic fibrosis (CF) treatment.

This period was characterized by significant advancements in clinical care and a growing body of literature on CF transmembrane conductance regulator (CFTR) modulators. These therapies have profoundly changed CF management, offering systemic benefits and establishing new guidelines.

Adapting Standards & Addressing Global Inequities

Updated Standards of Care (ECFS)

  • Guidelines updated across 4 key areas: diagnosis, health maintenance, CF-specific issues, planning for longer life.
  • 3 of these articles published in 2024.
  • Emphasis on a multidisciplinary and holistic approach.

Redefining Care Models (CFF)

  • Advocacy for interdisciplinary collaboration.
  • New roles: genetic counsellors, mental health coordinators.
  • Integration of telemedicine and shared decision-making.

The Transformative Impact of CFTR Modulators (ETI)

CFTRm therapies, particularly elexacaftor-tezacaftor-ivacaftor (ETI), have demonstrated broad therapeutic potential across multiple organ systems.

Expanding Eligibility: Hope for More Patients

  • Off-label ETI study (35 pwCF with at least one N1303K variant) showed a median reduction in sweat chloride (SwCl) of 9 mmol/L and a percent predicted forced expiratory volume in 1s (ppFEV1) improvement of 17%.
  • The expanded French compassionate program found that more than half of the CF population in France without F508del variants responded to ETI.
  • An in vitro study showed ETI increased chloride transport activity to ≥10% of normal CFTR function in 152 unapproved CFTR variants.

Re-evaluating Treatment Burdens: A Personalized Approach

The significant benefits of CFTR modulators have prompted crucial discussions on reducing the daily treatment burden, emphasizing a shift towards personalized care strategies.

SIMPLIFY-MCC Study Findings:

Discontinuing hypertonic saline or dornase alfa was found to be non-inferior to continuing treatment. Notably, discontinuation of dornase alfa resulted in significant improvements in MCC.

SIMPLIFY Study (pwCF with ppFEV1 >60% on ETI):

For patients with preserved lung function (ppFEV1 > 60%) on ETI, discontinuing daily hypertonic saline or dornase alfa for 6 weeks did not result in clinically significant changes in lung function.

Real-World De-escalation Success:

One center's algorithm led to 139 out of 174 participants reducing at least one adjunctive therapy over a year. Half of these patients achieved a 50% reduction in supportive therapies without any deterioration in patient outcomes.

Holistic Care: Mental Health & Beyond

Recent research increasingly addresses the comprehensive well-being of people with CF, encompassing physical, mental, financial, and social aspects.

Overall Improvements with CFTRm:

Most pwCF on CFTR modulators reported improvements in overall health, quality of life, and both physical and social well-being.

Mental Health: A Mixed Picture

  • Mental well-being reported to still deteriorate at similar rates regardless of CFTRm use.
  • Anxiety scores improved by 20%, depression by 30%.
  • However, 10% exhibited worse scores, often those with preexisting psychiatric diagnoses.
Adherence to CFTRm
73-89%
Mean adherence rates appeared better than traditional CF therapies but were still suboptimal.

Glimpses into the Future of CF Care: Innovations on the Horizon

There is substantial hope for the future, particularly for individuals who do not currently benefit from existing CFTR modulators.

Personalized Therapy Advances:

  • Patient-derived intestinal organoids (PDIOs) show promise for in vitro, patient-specific drug testing.
  • Pharmacokinetics studies highlight significant variability in drug concentrations, emphasizing the critical need for personalized dosing strategies.

AI-Based Diagnostics:

AI-based chest CT results are proving highly sensitive in detecting and monitoring structural lung disease, showing a reduction in bronchial wall thickness in young children.

New Drug Classes Explored:

Renewed exploration of epithelial sodium channel blockers is underway, offering potential new avenues for treatment.

RNA & DNA Targeting Progress:

  • Progress is being made with novel readthrough inducers (e.g., TLN468).
  • Development of third-generation lentiviral vectors.
  • Patient recruitment for gene therapy trials starting in 2024.

Conclusions & Key Takeaways

  • Multifaceted Efficacy of CFTRm: A growing body of evidence supports the broad and significant impact of CFTR modulators.
  • Evolving Care Models: The changing landscape of CF necessitates a fundamental shift in care models, requiring integration of diverse specialties and digital health technologies.
  • Personalization is Key: Therapy personalization, including careful assessment of CFTRm dosages and adjunctive therapies, is vital, as a single treatment model may not suit every patient.
  • Addressing Disparities: Global disparities in access to comprehensive care and CFTR modulators remain a significant challenge that must be urgently addressed.
  • Optimistic Outlook: The development of new drug classes and innovative RNA- and DNA-based treatments holds the potential to unlock new frontiers in CF care.
  • Fusion of Science & Equity: Overcoming challenges and seizing opportunities will require the fusion of innovative science with equitable healthcare delivery worldwide.
